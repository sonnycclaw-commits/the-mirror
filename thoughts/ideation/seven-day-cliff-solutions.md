# 7-Day Cliff ‚Äî Ideation Canvas

**Problem (Old):** ~~Users invest 7 days of psychological discomfort (anti-vision excavation) before receiving tangible value (Birth Chart).~~

**Problem (Reframed):** The 7-day experience was framed as "work." It should be framed as "conversation." The Birth Chart should feel like a *surprise reveal* of what TARS noticed ‚Äî not a reward for completing tasks.

**Insight:** Spotify Wrapped works because users just listen to music. The reveal is surprising because they didn't know they were generating data. S.T.A.R.S. should work the same way.

## Passive Signal Extraction (The Math Behind Wrapped)

TARS silently captures 5 signal types during conversation:

| Signal Type | Captured From | Example Reveal |
|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| **STATED** | Goals, fears, beliefs in words | "You said 'family first' 4 times" |
| **EMOTIONAL** | Sentiment, markers ("but...", "I can't...") | "Resistance appears when you mention money" |
| **TEMPORAL** | Response time, hesitation, session timing | "You pause longest on relationship questions" |
| **RELATIONAL** | People mentioned, sentiment toward them | "Partner mentioned 12 times, avg sentiment -0.3" |
| **BEHAVIORAL** | Topics skipped, deflections | "You avoided the career question twice" |

**Pattern Detection (Granger Causality):**
After 3+ observations, TARS detects sequences:
> "Every time you mention [person], your response time on the next question increases. There's a connection there."

## Anti-Patterns (Avoid)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Pure gamification** | Makes psychological depth feel like a game |
| **Nagging notifications** | Creates resentment, not engagement |
| **"You're falling behind"** | Shame ‚Üí avoidance ‚Üí churn |
| **Information overload** | Cognitive fatigue ‚Üí abandonment |
| **Promising too much** | Overhype ‚Üí disappointment at reveal |

## Promising Combinations

### Combo A: Preview-First
- Day 0: Show example Birth Chart ("This is what's coming")
- Day 1: First star appears immediately after first session
- Day 3: 30% of constellation visible, rest blurred
- Day 5: Dark star teased
- Day 7: Full reveal

### Combo B: Daily Stars
- Each day, one star appears based on that day's excavation
- By Day 7, all 7+ stars are visible
- Birth Chart is the CONNECTIONS revealing, not the stars themselves
- Stars without connections = Scattered phase

### Combo C: Sprint + Slow
- Day 1: 30-minute intensive ‚Üí first 3 stars appear
- Days 2-6: One question per day (low friction)
- Day 7: Deep synthesis session ‚Üí full constellation
- Tradeoff: Less psychological depth mid-week

### Combo D: Social Proof-First
- Day 0: "Join 10,000 people who discovered their Birth Chart"
- Day 1: Match with anonymous journey partner
- Daily: "Your partner completed Day 3. You're on Day 2."
- Day 7: Compare Birth Charts (anonymous or named)

### ‚≠ê Combo E: Wrapped-Style (RECOMMENDED)
- **Framing:** "Talk to TARS for 7 days. On Day 7, discover what TARS noticed."
- **Day 1:** Conversation (not quiz). First star appears. TARS: "Something caught my eye."
- **Days 2-6:** Conversation continues. TARS drops observations:
  - "You mentioned [X] 3 times. That's not random."
  - "I'm noticing a pattern. We'll explore it on Day 7."
- **Day 7:** Full reveal ‚Äî not as reward, but as *surprise gift*
  - "Here's what I've noticed about you..."
  - Patterns user didn't consciously provide
  - Granger causality reveals: "When [A], you [B]"

**Why this wins:**
- User doesn't feel like they're "doing work"
- Birth Chart feels like insight, not certificate
- TARS feels intelligent, not bureaucratic
- Matches the proven Spotify Wrapped psychology

*Combo E is the synthesis. The 7-day cliff dissolves when users don't know they're climbing it.*
